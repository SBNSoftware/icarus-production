Notes

  • User Activity: A significant increase in activity from Jacob Larkin was observed. The team concluded this was expected and related to his ongoing plot production, deeming it a temporary resource allocation and not a cause for concern.
  • Disk Space Usage: The team identified a more pressing issue with declining disk space.
  • A downward trend in free disk space (dcache persistent usage) was noted as a primary warning sign requiring close monitoring.
  • The team discussed the potential consequence: should the situation become critical, it would be necessary to delete older production datasets to free up capacity for new work.
  • Giuseppe’s analysis further clarified the challenge, noting that with V9 work unfinished and V10 campaigns starting, the team is in a "tight situation" for disk space.
  • To address this, Giuseppe initiated a disk usage (du) scan to identify deletable data, with results expected within a day.
  • Memory issue on overlay production: The team addressed a significant performance bottleneck caused by memory-intensive "overlay" production, which necessitated a strategic shift in how computing resources are allocated to meet project timelines.
  • The core problem identified is that overlay production campaigns are proceeding very slowly due to their large memory requirements, a known issue for V10 Monte Carlo work. This makes it difficult to secure the necessary multi-CPU slots, threatening the team's goal of completing these campaigns by the end of the year.
  • To address this bottleneck, the team formulated a new resource allocation strategy designed to guarantee consistent processing for the most demanding jobs
  • Dedicated Resources for Overlays: The team will reserve the dedicated 70% quota of the eos per user within the pro subgroup exclusively for the high-memory overlay jobs to ensure steady, consistent processing.
  • Opportunistic Resources for Other Campaigns: Correspondingly, all other, non-overlay campaigns will be moved off the pro subgroup to primarily use opportunistic slots, which are still a "sizable amount" and sufficient for those lower-memory jobs.
  • Parallel Optimizations: In parallel, efforts are underway to improve memory usage. One potential solution is splitting photon simulation from TPC simulation, which could reduce memory requirements from 16GB to 12GB and significantly ease resource contention.
  • Numi Campaign Offsite Execution: A proposal was raised to force all NuMI campaigns to run exclusively "offsite." The rationale was to guarantee they use opportunistic slots, freeing up all local resources. However, it was argued that after the primary resource segmentation is implemented, these campaigns would naturally run on opportunistic slots anyway, making this additional restriction unnecessary. The team concluded to hold off on forcing NuMI campaigns offsite for now but agreed to monitor their performance closely after the main changes are in place.
  • Extra Computing Resources: The final portion of the meeting addressed an important administrative task required to ensure the continuity of computing resources for the team. The team noted that the temporary increase in its available computing slots is set to expire at the end of October. The team agreed to contact CSAID to request an extension, with the ultimate goal of making the increase permanent at a future FCSG meeting.
